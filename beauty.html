BeautyKeep:
Mobile App for Organizing Beauty Products

The Problem

For beauty lovers, it is not easy to track their makeup products. This causes problems include buying duplicate products, not fully utilizing a product before it expires, and wasting time on hunting for the perfect product.

My Role

Product Designer

The Competitive Landscape

I analyzed websites and mobile apps that have the functionality of tracking beauty products but serve different purposes. Some weaknesses occur frequently in current solutions:

  • Incomplete databases that miss many popular products
  • Database does not allow user input and is not up-to-date
  • For those that allow users to input product information, inputting process is very tedious
  • Database not searchable or inefficient search feature
  • Cannot sort or filter products in a user-friendly way

User Needs

I interviewed four people with different levels of knowledge and interest in beauty products and developed three distinct personas to understand the population better. I discovered that people have different number of products, different level of knowledge about makeup, and different expectations for a beauty product managing app. Expert users care more about tracking the collection while people who do not own many products are very interested in seeing what other people are using and how they use them. At the end of the day, users all want to know what products will be most suitable for them and make them more beautiful. These findings showed that the product would be much more useful if it had sharing feature and a community component. Therefore I decided that there should be usergenerated content such as ratings and tags, and users should be able to share how they use the products in real life.

Ideation

I explored various solutions with sketches, and used storyboards to illustrate user's interactions with different solutions. In the storyboards, I thought through the process of adding products easily and sharing product reviews.

Prototyping and Testing

First Iteration: Paper Prototype

I first made a cheap paper prototype to quickly test my ideas. For fast entry of product information, I want to allow users to scan barcodes or packages and search in a database. The system would also allow user-generated content so that the database can always up-to-date.
The main screen is trends and recommendations personalized based on user data, so that people can save time on finding the perfect products for them.
Users can sort and filter products with a wide range of criteria. This meets the needs of users who want to manage a relatively large amount of products and discover more information from large amount or products.
Users can also virtually try on all the products that are in their collection and wish list, in order to create their own looks to share with others or just see the effect. They can also upload photos to share their looks.

Second Interation and High-fi Prototype

The feedback on the paper prototype revealed that the traditional way of showing products in a thumbnail view and allowing users to create their favorite list feels old. The virtual try-on feature, though being a "cool" idea, does not serve the purpose of sharing and learning about products but is more helpful for a shopping app.
I decided to have a chatbot, which is a very efficient way to flatten the information architecture and simplify user tasks. In the same spirit, I designed small customizable cards that can display meaningful information for different people.
I incorporated these ideas in my wireframes, and finally, a high-fidelity prototype.
