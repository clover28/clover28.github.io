Musi:
Foot-controlled recording device, CHI 2018 Semi-finalist

The Problem

Practice is a critical part of classical musicians' life. Solo practice is an important way for musicians to improve their technique and reflect on their performance, but that time alone can lead to frustration and discouragement. Group rehearsal, on the other hand, provides musicians with support and motivation, but is hard to coordinate. We seeks a solution that helps classical musicians practice, reflect, and coordinate with peers at ease.

My Contribution

This is a project for a course themed pervasive interaction design. I was involved in the design and execution of the initial research, including observation, interviews, and cultural probe. I sketched the idea of a foot-controller that helps loop music, and it later became our main idea. We made low-fidelity prototype of the product and conducted user enactments to get feedback. I designed the appearance of our final product and created the physical prototype together with Shu. I also prototyped our companion mobile app.
